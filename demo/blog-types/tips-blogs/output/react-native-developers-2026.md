## Tip 1: Expo Experience and Bare Workflow Are Two Different Resumes

Most React Native developers learned through Expo's managed workflow. That works for MVPs with standard features. It breaks down when your product needs custom native integrations, Bluetooth, biometrics, or background processing. None of these are supported by the Expo managed workflow.

Bare workflow developers write native code, understand the JavaScript-to-native bridge, and debug across the platform layer. Expo-only developers cannot do this without substantial ramp-up. Bare developers can always drop into an Expo environment; the reverse is rarely true.

**Ask this**: "Walk me through a time you had to eject from Expo or work outside the managed workflow. What broke and how did you fix it?"

A candidate who has only done Expo projects will not have a confident answer.

## Tip 3: App Store Links Are the First Filter, Not the Last

One of the most direct tips to hire React Native developers: require live App Store or Google Play links before scheduling any call.

Shipping a production app means navigating store submission, handling rejection cycles, managing versioning, and keeping an app stable through OS updates. None of this can be faked in a tutorial.

- Ask for apps they built or contributed to directly
- Install the app and test transitions, offline behavior, and cold start performance
- Check the last update date. An app abandoned two-plus years ago signals low long-term ownership

**Ask this**: "What was your most recent App Store or Play Store rejection, and how did you resolve it?"

## Tip 4: Native Module Experience Costs 20 Percent More and Prevents Most Rewrites

Developers who write custom Swift and Kotlin bridges earn a 15 to 20 percent salary premium above standard rates. That premium is easy to skip when budget is tight.

When your app needs device-level integrations like NFC, advanced camera controls, or biometric APIs, a developer without native module experience hits the ceiling fast. The workaround is usually a costly rewrite or months of delays.

**Example**: A fintech team hired mid-level React Native developers to cut costs. When biometric login required Kotlin-level code, none of them could build it. The fix cost more than the salary delta would have across the full engagement.

## Tip 6: React.js on a Resume Is Not React Native Experience

Many hiring managers see "React" and assume mobile competence. React.js developers build web interfaces with HTML and CSS. React Native developers build mobile apps with native components, handle platform-specific rendering, and manage App Store and Play Store submissions.

These are different roles. A senior React.js developer cannot substitute for a senior React Native developer without a significant ramp.

**Ask this**: "How do you handle rendering differences between iOS and Android in the same codebase?" A web-first developer will not have a concrete, practical answer.

## Tip 7: State Management Choice Reveals Architecture Thinking Better Than Any Whiteboard Test

Ask a candidate to explain why they chose Redux, Zustand, Context API, or MobX for a specific project. An experienced developer will describe trade-offs grounded in team size, data complexity, and scale. A less experienced one will say "I always use Redux."

This is one of the most overlooked tips to hire React Native developers: architectural judgment does not show up on a resume or in algorithm questions.

**Ask**: "What would you change about the state management in your last production app, and why?"

Thoughtful critique signals mature engineering judgment. Defensive answers signal someone who learned patterns without understanding them.

## Tip 8: For Remote Hires, Async Communication Is a Technical Skill

To hire remote React Native developers well, evaluate async communication before the technical screen, not after.

Send a written prompt asking the candidate to explain a trade-off they made in a recent project. Evaluate:
- Is the answer structured and specific, or vague and padded?
- Does it demonstrate engineering judgment without prompting?
- Is it appropriately concise?

Developers who cannot communicate clearly in writing create friction across every PR review, sprint update, and architecture discussion. For cross platform developers in the USA hiring remote teams, this screen catches more mismatches than technical tests alone.

## Tip 9: Offshore Rates Are 40 to 60 Percent Lower, and the Quality Gap Is Mostly a Myth

[Eastern European and Latin American developers](https://www.remotecrew.io/blog/hire-react-native-developers) deliver React Native work at 40 to 60 percent of US rates. Senior developers in Poland, Romania, Brazil, and Argentina typically run $65,000 to $100,000 annually versus $140,000 to $185,000 in the USA.

Timezone works in your favor: Eastern Europe overlaps US mornings and Latin America overlaps most of the US workday.

The quality myth persists because offshore hiring done poorly looks like an offshore problem. It is almost always a scoping problem. A clear technical spec covering Expo vs. Bare, must-have skills, and platform scope resolves the majority of quality issues regardless of geography.

## Tip 10: Dedicated Over Freelance When the Project Runs Past 90 Days

Freelancers context-switch between clients. Every time an engagement resumes after a gap, onboarding cost is paid again. Institutional knowledge covering edge cases, product decisions, and architecture history does not transfer reliably in a handoff document.

Dedicated developers retain context across the full project lifecycle. They flag regressions before they ship, and they accelerate as the codebase matures. For React Native mobile app services spanning multiple quarters, the economics of dedicated developers are unambiguous.

**Pro Tip**: Calculate re-onboarding cost before signing a freelance contract for anything longer than 90 days. It is nearly always higher than the rate difference.
