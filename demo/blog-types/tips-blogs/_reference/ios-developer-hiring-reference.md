## Structural Patterns (Use the Shape, Not the Language)

- Tips 1–2 are always process/model decisions (before you start)
- Tips 3–5 are portfolio and technical evaluation
- Tips 6–7 are domain-specific compliance and communication testing
- Tips 8–9 are post-hire considerations (support, references)
- Tip 10 is the soft-skills closer
- FAQ topics: cost, model differences, technical skills for [role], remote hiring, [domain-specific question]

---

## Language Patterns to AVOID (Do Not Mirror These)

- "Finding the right X sounds manageable until you are two months in and realize..."
- "This happens often, and it happens because..."
- "One of the most practical tips to hire X is making the model decision before you start screening."
- "A developer with real experience will ask clarifying questions first, expected user volume, offline requirements..."
- "How to test this: describe your app's [context] and ask the candidate to walk through [topic]. A developer with real experience answers without hesitation."
- "Every architectural redo, missed [milestone], and delayed launch traces back to a hiring decision made with the wrong criteria."
- "If you are planning an X engagement and want to get the hiring decision right the first time, let's talk."

These sentences work. They are just already used. Do not repeat them.
