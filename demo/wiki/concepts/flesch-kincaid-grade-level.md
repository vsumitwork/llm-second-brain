# Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

The companion metric to [[flesch-reading-ease]]. Maps text difficulty directly to US school grade levels. A grade level of 9 means the reader needs approximately 9 years of education to understand the text comfortably.

## Formula

```
Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level = 0.39 × (Words / Sentences) + 11.8 × (Syllables / Words) − 15.59
```

## Grade Level Targets for WildnetEdge Content

| Content Type | Target Grade Level | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Blogs | Grade 8–10 | Professional but accessible to all decision-makers |
| Service Pages | Grade 8–10 | Same audience; clarity drives conversion |
| Newsletters | Grade 7–9 | Slightly more casual; inbox context |
| LinkedIn Posts | Grade 7–8 | Short, punchy; lower complexity needed |

A grade level of 8–10 corresponds approximately to [[flesch-reading-ease]] score of 50–70 — aligning with the user's 50+ requirement.

## Relationship to Flesch Reading Ease

| Flesch Grade Level | Approx. Flesch Reading Ease |
|---|---|
| Grade 5–6 | 80–100 (Very easy) |
| Grade 7–8 | 70–80 (Fairly easy) |
| Grade 8–10 | 50–70 (Standard to fairly difficult) |
| Grade 11–12 | 30–50 (Difficult) |
| College+ | 0–30 (Very confusing) |
