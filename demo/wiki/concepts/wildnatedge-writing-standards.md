# WildnetEdge Writing Standards

The distilled rules for all [[wildnet-edge]] content. Two sources combine: this writing guide (tone, structure, brand voice) and [[central-rag-document]] (scoring, SEO mechanics, GEO). **Both must be satisfied.**

## Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No em dashes in blogs.** This is an explicit brand constraint. Use commas, colons, or sentence breaks instead.
2. **Never assume beginner-level knowledge.** Audience is CTOs, Founders, Digital Heads — decision-maker level.
3. **Every piece must influence a decision.** If it doesn't help the reader understand a problem, evaluate options, reduce uncertainty, or move toward action — don't publish it.
4. **No generic educational filler.** Every section must provide original insight, real examples, or actionable takeaways.
5. **AI is a drafting partner, not an author.** Inject domain reasoning, refine tone, verify claims. Do not publish raw AI output.
6. **[[flesch-reading-ease]] score must be 50+.** Target 60–70 for ideal readability.

## Tone

**Mandatory:** Conversational, Insightful, Confident, Clear, Human, Practical

**Avoid:** Overly academic language, corporate buzzword stacking, sales-heavy messaging, empty claims, robotic phrasing

**Example correction:**
- ❌ "We are the best experts in..."
- ✓ "Organizations often face... We help address this by..."

## Sentence & Paragraph Rules

- Prefer active voice
- Vary sentence length — don't let consecutive sentences all be the same length
- Avoid complex nested clauses
- **Paragraphs: 3–5 lines maximum** — one idea per paragraph
- Maintain narrative flow — each paragraph should lead logically to the next

## [[keyword-placement-priority]] Checklist

Primary keyword must appear in:
- [ ] Title / H1
- [ ] First 100 words
- [ ] At least one H2 heading
- [ ] Meta title
- [ ] Meta description
- [ ] URL slug
- [ ] Conclusion

Where appropriate: image alt text, FAQ section

## [[keyword-density-targets]]

- Primary keyword: **6–10 uses per 1,000 words** (0.8–1.2% density; max 1.5%)
- Secondary keywords: **2–4 uses each**
- Semantic keywords: natural inclusion only
- Adjust proportionally for longer pieces
- **Natural readability always overrides density targets**

## Content Length by Type

| Type | Target |
|------|--------|
| Blogs | 1,500–2,000 words |
| Service Pages | 1,500–2,000 words |
| Industry Pages | 1,400–2,000 words |
| Website Pages | 1,200–1,500 words |
| Newsletters | 800–1,200 words |
| Case Studies | 500–800 words |
| LinkedIn Posts | 20–50 words |
| Instagram Captions | 50–150 words |

## Internal Linking Standard

Each piece: **3–6 internal links minimum** — service pages, industry pages, related blog content (blogs only).

## Blog-Specific Requirements

From [[2026-06-10-ios-developer-hiring-blog]] as reference pattern:
- Open with a specific failure scenario or tension — stakes make the reader stay
- Use cost/comparison tables where data supports them
- Include 5–8 FAQ questions at end (also improves AIO extraction)
- End with a direct CTA that links to the relevant service page
- Subheadings should integrate primary keyword or strong semantic variations

## Brand Positioning to Maintain in Every Piece

- WildnetEdge operates at the strategy + execution intersection
- AI-first mindset
- Business outcomes, not features
- Faster decision cycles and reduced dependency on paid channels
- Long-term distribution advantage through AI/organic presence
