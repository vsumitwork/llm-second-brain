# Blog Content Pattern

The structural template for [[wildnet-edge]] blog posts, derived from [[wildnatedge-writing-standards]] and validated by [[2026-06-10-ios-developer-hiring-blog]] as the reference example.

## Standard Blog Structure

1. **Frontmatter** (Obsidian/CMS metadata): title, source URL, published date, description, tags
2. **Key Takeaways box** — 5 bullet points at the very top. Front-loads value for decision-makers who skim first.
3. **Hook paragraph** — a specific failure scenario, tension, or problem that makes the reader stay. Stakes-first.
4. **Data table or cost table** (where applicable) — concrete numbers anchor the piece for ROI-focused readers.
5. **Core sections (H2s)** — each section is one actionable point, 1–3 paragraphs. Follow the [[answer-capsule-framework]].
6. **FAQ section** — 5–8 Q&As. Each labeled sequentially. Directly answers long-tail search questions. Improves [[ai-overviews-aio]] extraction.
7. **CTA** — one line of context, one hook statement, one action label. Links to relevant [[wildnet-edge]] service page.

## Mandatory Characteristics

- **Reader-centric:** Every section answers the reader's next question, not a topic the writer wanted to cover.
- **Solution-oriented:** Frame everything around solving a problem or removing a risk.
- **Value-dense:** No filler. Every sentence earns its place.
- **Active voice.** No em dashes.
- **Clear narrative:** each section flows logically to the next.

## Internal Linking Pattern (from reference blog)

- Weave 3–6 internal links into natural anchor text within body paragraphs
- Link to relevant service pages, related blog guides, and industry-specific pages
- Never use "click here" as anchor text — use descriptive, keyword-rich phrases

## Length & Quality Gate

- Target: 1,500–2,000 words
- [[wildnatedge-writing-standards]] scorecard minimum: 4.0/5.0 average
- [[content-scoring-rubric]] minimum: 75/100 before publishing
- [[flesch-reading-ease]] minimum: 50+ (target 60+)

## Opening Hook Technique (from reference blog)

Lead with a specific failure scenario that the target reader has experienced or fears:
> "Finding the right iOS developer sounds manageable until you are two months in and realize the person you hired has never shipped a production app to the App Store..."

This pattern: name the problem → add the cost/consequence → make the reader nod before the first tip.
