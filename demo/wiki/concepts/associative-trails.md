# Associative Trails

[[vannevar-bush]]'s term from "As We May Think" (1945) for persistent, named, shareable linked paths through a collection of documents in the [[memex]]. The idea that the **connections between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves**.

## Bush's Original Vision

A trail is a sequence of items linked by association — the way the human mind actually works (by association, not by hierarchical index). In the Memex, you could forge a trail through any set of items, name it, save it, and share it with others. Others could follow your trail and fork their own.

## Modern Equivalent

In the [[llm-wiki-pattern]], associative trails are implemented as `[[wikilinks]]` — every cross-reference between pages is a persistent, navigable trail. The LLM maintains these trails automatically; the human can follow them in [[obsidian]]'s graph view.

The graph view in Obsidian is the closest visual equivalent to Bush's vision of seeing the trails through your knowledge base.
