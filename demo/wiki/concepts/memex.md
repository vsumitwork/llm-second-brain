# Memex

A hypothetical personal knowledge device proposed by [[vannevar-bush]] in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." The Memex is a desk-sized device using microfilm to store a person's books, records, and communications, with the ability to create and traverse **[[associative-trails]]** between items.

## Key Properties

- **Personal and private** — individual curation, not shared/public like the web became.
- **Associative trails** — linked paths through documents that can be saved, named, and shared. The connections are as valuable as the documents.
- **Active curation** — the owner manually files, links, and annotates. The Memex provides the infrastructure; the human does the maintenance.

## Historical Significance

- Anticipated hypertext (Ted Nelson, 1960s) and the World Wide Web by decades.
- Bush's vision was closer to a **personal curated wiki** than to what the web became (public, link-spam-prone, algorithmically surfaced).

## Relationship to LLM Wiki

The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is the modern implementation of Bush's vision. The critical difference: Bush couldn't solve who does the maintenance. An LLM does it — never bored, never forgetting to update a cross-reference.
