# Google Helpful Content System (HCS)

A site-wide, continuously updated ML classifier that identifies and suppresses content created primarily for search engines rather than for humans. Unlike older Panda updates, **HCS is a persistent signal** — a site predominantly hosting unhelpful content carries a sitewide ranking penalty even if individual pages are strong.

## The "Who, How, Why" Evaluation

**WHO** created the content? Is there a credible, identified author with a track record?

**HOW** was it produced? Human expert research with editorial oversight, or machine-generated without review?

**WHY** was it created? To serve the user's information need — or to capture keyword rankings with no unique value?

## Positive Signals (What HCS Rewards)

- Answers the specific query completely and accurately
- Provides value beyond what top-ranking pages cover
- Written from real experience or deep subject-matter expertise
- References original data, proprietary research, or unique case examples
- Logical structure: introduction → problem → solution → evidence → conclusion
- Appropriate length for query type — not padded, not truncated
- Cites and links to high-quality external sources
- Regularly updated; author clearly identified

## Negative Signals (HCS Targets)

- Covers a topic only because it has search volume — not because the site has genuine expertise
- Extensive AI content generation without human expert review
- Summarises what other sources say without adding original perspective
- Excessive keyword repetition that reads unnaturally
- **Thin content:** 300–500 words for topics that warrant 2,000+
- Clickbait titles that over-promise vs. content that under-delivers
- Doorway pages: near-duplicate articles targeting geographic keyword variants
- Programmatic SEO pages without unique editorial content per page

## HCS Self-Assessment Checklist

Before publishing any WildnetEdge content, confirm:
- [ ] Does it provide original information, reporting, research, or analysis?
- [ ] Does it provide a substantial, complete description of the topic?
- [ ] Does it offer insightful analysis beyond the obvious?
- [ ] Does the headline match what the content actually delivers?
- [ ] Is it the sort of page you would bookmark, cite, or share?
- [ ] Would someone feel they learned enough to accomplish their goal?
- [ ] Would a reader feel they need to go elsewhere for a better answer? (should be no)
- [ ] Is it free of spelling/stylistic errors?
- [ ] Is it produced by experts who demonstrably know the topic?

## Relationship to Other Standards

HCS violations directly reduce [[e-e-a-t]] scores and lower the [[content-scoring-rubric]] score, especially in the "Content Quality & Structure" category (20/100 points). The [[wildnatedge-writing-standards]] hard rule "never write generic educational filler" is a direct HCS compliance measure.
