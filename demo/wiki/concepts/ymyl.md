# YMYL — Your Money or Your Life

Content that, if inaccurate or misleading, could have significant negative consequences for the reader's health, financial stability, safety, or wellbeing. Google applies the **highest [[e-e-a-t]] standards** to YMYL pages — formal credentials are mandatory, not optional.

## YMYL Content Categories

- Health and medical: symptoms, diagnoses, treatments, medications, mental health, nutrition
- Financial: investing, tax advice, loans, insurance, retirement planning
- Legal: contracts, rights, immigration, criminal law, family law
- Safety: emergency procedures, chemical hazards, product safety
- Government and civic: voting, benefits, legal rights
- Major life decisions: education, career, housing, childcare

## YMYL Mandatory Requirements

1. Author must hold formal, relevant credentials (MD, JD, CFP, CPA, etc.)
2. Content must be reviewed — and the review noted on the page
3. Citations must link to primary, peer-reviewed, or government sources
4. Claims must be hedged conservatively: "consult a professional"
5. Update frequency higher — quarterly or upon any guideline change
6. No affiliate links that could create a conflict of interest
7. Contact details for the professional/organisation must be verifiable

## Relevance to WildnetEdge

WildnetEdge primarily creates B2B technology content — **most topics are not YMYL.** However, if content ventures into fintech, healthcare technology, or legal-tech domains, YMYL standards must apply. When in doubt, apply YMYL requirements.
