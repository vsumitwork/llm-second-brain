# WildnetEdge Content Writing & SEO Guide

**Source:** `raw/WildnetEdge_Writing_Guide.md`
**Ingested:** 2026-06-10

## Summary

The universal writing and SEO standard for all [[wildnet-edge]] content channels — website pages, service pages, blogs, newsletters, social media, case studies, and email. Defines tone, SEO execution, content-type formats, conversion writing, and quality scorecards. This document is the brand voice and execution standard; the [[central-rag-document]] is the scoring and strategy standard. Both must be satisfied.

## Key Claims

- **Every piece must help the reader** understand a problem, evaluate options, reduce uncertainty, or move toward action. If it doesn't influence a decision, don't publish it.
- **Target audience:** CTOs, Product Leaders, Digital Heads, Startup Founders, Enterprise Stakeholders. Time-constrained, ROI-focused, technology-aware, solution-driven. **Never assume beginner-level knowledge.**
- **Brand positioning:** WildnetEdge operates at the strategy + execution intersection; AI-first; business outcomes not features; compounding selection probability via AI presence.
- **Blogs must avoid em dashes.** Active voice, varied sentence length, 3–5 line paragraphs.
- **[[keyword-density-targets]]:** Primary keyword 6–10 uses per 1,000 words (0.8–1.2% density; max 1.5%). Secondary: 2–4 uses each. Natural readability overrides density.
- **[[keyword-placement-priority]]:** Primary keyword must appear in H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta title, meta description, URL slug, and conclusion.
- **Internal linking:** 3–6 links minimum per piece — service pages, industry pages, related blog content.
- **No em dashes in blogs.** (Repeated rule — treated as a hard constraint.)
- **AI is a drafting partner, not an author** — inject domain reasoning, refine tone, verify claims. Publishing raw AI output is not acceptable.
- **Blog length:** 1,500–2,000 words. Service pages: 1,500–2,000. Industry pages: 1,400–2,000. Website pages: 1,200–1,500. Newsletters: 800–1,200. Case studies: 500–800.
- **Final approval threshold:** Blogs and service pages require average scorecard score of 4.0/5.0. Website pages and social: 3.5/5.0.
