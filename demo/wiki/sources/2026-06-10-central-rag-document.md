## Summary

A comprehensive 20-section reference document for scoring content against SEO, [[e-e-a-t]], [[geo-llm-visibility]], page-type standards, and advanced content strategy. This is the primary scoring and standards engine for all content produced. Every piece of content should be evaluated against the [[content-scoring-rubric]] defined here.

## Key Claims

- **[[e-e-a-t]] is not a direct ranking signal** — it shapes the systems (Helpful Content System, SpamBrain) that suppress or reward at scale. Trust is the master dimension; the other three feed into it.
- **[[google-helpful-content-system]] (HCS)** is site-wide — one domain full of unhelpful content tanks even strong individual pages.
- **[[ai-overviews-aio]]** appear on 25.8% of all US queries; 70% of B2B tech queries; 65.9% of long-tail (7+ word) queries. 44% of citations come from the top 30% of page content.
- **[[geo-llm-visibility]]** is an extension of SEO, not a replacement. All foundational SEO signals are prerequisite. LLM traffic predicted to overtake Google search by late 2027 (Semrush 2026).
- **[[answer-capsule-framework]]** — every major section should be a self-contained extractable unit: question-form H2, 1-2 sentence direct answer, supporting bullets, 1 statistic with source.
- **[[flesch-reading-ease]]** target for body copy: score of 60+ (standard to fairly easy). Achievable by short sentences, simple words, active voice.
- **[[content-scoring-rubric]]** — 100-point scale: E-E-A-T (25), Content Quality (20), Keyword/Intent (15), LLM Visibility (15), Page-Type Best Practices (15), Technical Baseline (10). Score 75+ before publishing.
- **[[keyword-intent-mapping]]** — four intent types: Informational, Navigational, Commercial, Transactional. Mismatching content type to intent is one of the most common and costly SEO errors.
- Fact density matters: sourced statistics cited **40% more often** by LLMs than opinion-only content (Princeton GEO Study, ACM KDD 2024).
- Long-tail (7+ words) keywords trigger AI Overviews 65.9% of the time — build FAQ sections around these.
- **[[schema-markup]]** is the bridge between human-readable content and machine-processable knowledge — mandatory for Organization, Article, Author on every page.
- **[[ymyl]]** content requires formal credentials — medical, financial, legal, safety topics carry the highest E-E-A-T bar.
- Blog posts: 1,500–2,000+ words. Pillar posts: 2,500–5,000+. Every H2 section minimum 150 words.
- Update evergreen content annually minimum; fast-moving topics quarterly. Display "Last Updated" date.
