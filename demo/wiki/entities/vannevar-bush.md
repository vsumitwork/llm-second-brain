# Vannevar Bush

American engineer and science administrator (1890–1974). Director of the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during World War II. Best known in the context of knowledge management for his 1945 essay "As We May Think," which introduced the concept of the [[memex]].

## Relevance to This Wiki

Bush's intellectual legacy is the original inspiration for the [[llm-wiki-pattern]]. The pattern solves the one problem Bush could not: **who does the maintenance**. Bush envisioned a person manually filing and linking documents via microfilm. The LLM wiki replaces that human maintenance burden with an LLM that never gets bored and never forgets to update a cross-reference.

## Key Ideas

- **[[memex]]** (1945): a hypothetical personal knowledge device using microfilm, storing books, records, and communications, with the ability to create [[associative-trails]] between items.
- **[[associative-trails]]**: Bush's term for persistent linked paths through documents — the idea that the connections between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves.
- Anticipated hypertext and the web by decades, but envisioned something more **personal and curated** than what the web became.
